ECG Project

For my final undergraduate thesis project, I developed a Python application to automate the measurement of the P-wave's terminal component on digital ECGs. Due to its size, it is difficult and time consuming to accurately measure this by hand, and so automating the measurement provides a useful tool for researchers and clinicians.
Accomplishing this requires complex digital signal processing to determine waveform boundaries. An overview of each step and its result on the ECG are described in the demo, and can be visualized on a variety of ECGs with differing morphology.

Pathfinding

The pathfinding demo allows you to visualize A* or Dijkstra's algorithm on an open board or a randomly generated maze by highlighting expanded nodes. In either case, the user may alter the board by drawing or deleting walls and by moving the start or end point.
Random maze generation is done using Eller's algorithm, chosen for its speed and low memory cost. It is able to generate a perfect maze of infinite size in linear time, without having to observe more than a single row at once.
Diagonal movement is enabled, so an octile heuristic is used by A* rather than manhattan distance. The octile heuristic was used over euclidean distance because it takes into account that diagonal movement can only be 45Â°, resulting in fewer expanded nodes.

Sorting

The sorting demo allows you to visualize the progression of different sorting methods on rows of varying size. The visualization is done by highlighting the repositioned rows during each step. Implemented sorting methods are bubble sort, selection sort, insertion sort, merge sort, and quick sort.

AI Publication

From January to September of 2020, I worked with the Canadian Health Information Management Association (CHIMA) to co-author a paper on Artificial Intelligence in Healthcare. Topics include an introduction to artificial intelligence and machine learning, AI-based diagnostics, computer-assisted medical coding, and AI in data analytics. This paper was published October 2020, and is available online.
Brandon Lieng, BMSc, Brody Taylor, BMSc, Curtis Black, BMSc, & Sheraza Burney, BSc (2020). Professional Practice Briefing: Artificial Intelligence in Health Care. Canadian Health Information Management Association.
